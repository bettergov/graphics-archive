Timeline: How public costs for Jesse White Community Center more than doubled

$10M

Feb. 2010 Chicago Park District board approves a public-private partnership with the Jesse White Foundation for construction of a $15 million fieldhouse.

June 2013 Construction begins. Total construction estimated costs drop to $12.8 million, reducing the foundation’s share.

May 2014 White’s foundation receives a $1.5 million line of credit needed to finish construction from the First Bank of Highland Park.

Sept. 2014 Ribbon cutting ceremony to open the center.

May 2015 White’s foundation is refunded unspent construction funds, reducing total project cost to $12.2 million.

Nov. 2010 Chicago City Council approves $5 million in funding for the project using tax increment financing funds.

March 2011 Chicago City Council approves an additional $5 million in TIF spending for the center in an ordinance sponsored by Ald. Walter Burnett, 27th, a political protégé of White’s.
