Three ideas for police oversight

All three proposals create bodies that…

  • Conduct systematic reviews and make recommendations to improve policing and police accountability
  • Engage with community on police matters

1 Civilian Police Accountability Council

Powers

  • Approve CPD’s budget
  • Negotiate union contracts
  • Hear disciplinary matters
  • Investigate misconduct
  • Appoint superintendent
  • Oversee rules, regulations, and policies of police department
  • Issue subpoenas

Governing structure

Elected

Each police district elects one representative to a city-wide council, for a total of 22 representatives. The voting power of each representative equals the percentage of the city population in their district.

2 Grassroots Alliance for Police Accountability

Powers

  • Set priorities and policy for the CPD, Civilian Office of Police Accountability (COPA), and police board
  • Screen candidates for superintendent
  • Appoint head of COPA and police board members
  • Fire, for cause, superintendent, head of COPA, and police board members
  • Issue subpoenas

Governing structure

Partially elected

Each police district elects three representatives to serve on district councils, for a total of 66 representatives. A commission, made up of seven people appointed by district councils, has significant say in policing and police oversight.

3 Ald. Ariel Reboyras' proposal

Powers

  • Screen candidates for head of COPA
  • Provide input on the performance of the head of COPA and the deputy IG of public safety

Governing structure

Appointed

A mix of the mayor, City Council, and sitting commissioners appoint members to a city-wide commission made up of either nine or 21 representatives.
