Jason Van Dyke

Murder Defendant

Chicago Police Officer who shot Laquan McDonald

Van Dyke, 40, was a 13-year CPD veteran with dozens of commendations when he shot McDonald. He was an accomplished officer and frequent subject of excessive-force complaints. Apart from a $5 million city settlement with McDonald’s estate over that shooting, the city has been ordered to pay a total of nearly $450,000 in two lawsuits alleging he used excessive force.

Last updated: Aug. 26, 2018

Laquan McDonald

Deceased

Shot to death by Chicago Police Officer Jason Van Dyke

McDonald, 17, was a foster child who mostly lived with relatives. He spent much of his short life in a poor and violent area on Chicago’s West Side. Most of what we know about McDonald comes from official documents. He was arrested for drugs and spent time in juvenile detention. He often impressed officials who tried to help him. He had a knife and the hallucinogen PCP in his system when he was killed, officials said.

Tina Hunter

Mother of Laquan McDonald

Could be called to testify about her son’s “propensity for violence”

Hunter, 36, was a 15-year-old ward of the state when she gave birth to McDonald. She was trying to regain custody of her son during the last year of his life. She appeared in a closed hearing Aug. 16 about her son’s “propensity for violence,” but it remains unknown whether the judge would allow Van Dyke’s lawyers to call her to testify at trial.

Vincent Gaughan

Judge in the criminal division of Illinois Circuit Court of Cook County

Assigned to Officer Jason Van Dyke’s case

Gaughan, 77, is a former public defender who has been a judge since 1991 and presided over several high-profile cases. He is known for fairness, preparedness, erratic temperament, and secrecy. Chicago news outlets, including WBEZ Chicago and the Chicago Tribune, petitioned the Illinois Supreme Court for an order that increased public access to force him to release more information. Gaughan himself has been in trouble for firearm use: He was arrested in 1970 for nearly shooting four people, including two cops.

Joseph McMahon

Special prosecutor in the murder case against Officer Jason Van Dyke

Kane County State’s Attorney

McMahon, 52, was brought in just for this case after Cook County’s top prosecutor withdrew her office from it. Civil-rights advocates had alleged conflicts of interest between local prosecutors and the CPD officers they rely on for convictions. In March 2017, McMahon made the unusual move of convening a second grand jury, which added 16 aggravated battery counts. Later in the year, he publicly mulled running in the Republican primary for Illinois attorney general.

Photo: (Nancy Stone / Chicago Tribune)

Last updated: Sept. 14, 2018

Dan Herbert

Defense attorney for Officer Jason Van Dyke

Former police officer and prosecutor

Herbert, 50, a former staff attorney of the union that represents 10,000 Chicago police officers, leads Van Dyke’s legal team. He has attempted many times to get the charges against Van Dyke thrown out. Last year, he represented a Chicago officer convicted on federal civil-rights charges after firing 16 shots at a moving car and wounding two teens inside. That shooting was captured on video.
